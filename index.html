Our Popular Co-Curricular
Activities

SWIMMING

Swimming is a great workout because you need to move your whole body against the resistance of the water. This activity keeps your heart rate up but takes some of the impact stress off your body it also builds endurance, muscle strength and cardiovascular fitness amongst other healthy benefit. As part of the co - curricular activities in the school calendar, Jack and Jill School students take part in swimming competition within the county and with prospects to win almost every swimming competition seasons. This has been necessitated since with reference to the club of choice the children, pupils and students visit the swimming pools for both rehearsals or body exercises on Wednesday: Every other Week

CHESS

When playing chess, your brain is challenged to exercise logic, develop pattern recognition, make decisions both visually and analytically, and test your memory. Chess can be enjoyed by any age—as a result, these brain exercises can be part of the health of your brain for your entire life! Our school provide this activities in clubs: Chess Club. Students with low memory of remembrance are advised to practise this analytical game not for fun but rather for exercise pattern development and recognition and analysis, thereby sometimes improving the pupils cognitive skills.

GOLF

Golf is an individual sport played by hitting a ball with a club from a tee into a hole. A standard golf course consists of 18 holes, where each hole is labeled as Par 3, 4, 5, rarely a Par 6. “Par” in golf stands for the regulated amount of strokes it takes to get the ball into the hole. The idea of the game is to get the ball in the hole the fewest amount of strokes as possible. First and foremost, golf is good exercise. Not only do you spend a lot of time on your feet, but you also do a lot of walking. In fact, most courses will have you walking around 4 miles by the end, so you'll certainly get in your daily step goals. And whereas there's nothing wrong with playing golf with a friend, another benefit of playing golf is that you can make new friends.

SKATING

Skating (floor) is a workout sport played while wearing skating shoes or boots mainly on the floor or a smooth terrace. There are different types of skating including Jam skating; usually practised with song beats Inline Skating; this is the popular type of skating and Artistic or Ice Skating; this is practised on ice fields by normally artistic or artistes persons. At Jack and Jill School we practise the Inline Speed Skating. We have team leaders and coaches training learners during club time/hour on ways to and how to skate. Junior learners and beginners are always provided with a support coach or an expert student to help them gain confidence before starting the training. Our students/ learners are provided with safety tools and attires before entering the training or the skating fields, this helps is preventing injuries that may rise from mishandling or lack of concentration for beginners.
